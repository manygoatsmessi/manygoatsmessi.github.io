PURPOSE

Mars has always been a fascinating world. Its similarity to Earth in many aspects such as it's year length, tilt, temperature and distance from the Sun makes it an eye-catching place. These factors means that Mars is a perfect candidate for humanity's new home. Ares will be the first step in this venture. It will not only mark the beginning of a journey, but will also benifit the human race in many other ways.

MISSION

Ares will be the first step in human exploration of Mars. It will establish the first martian colonies, capable of holding the first crew to visit the red planet. It will feature the most advanced technology in the world, as well as a team full of highly trained specialists.

NASA IPSC

To get to Mars and other worlds beyond, we must design a spacecraft capable of deep space travel. It also requires an efficient engine. Using modern technologies, we will construct our newest spacecraft: the Interplanetary Spacecraft, or IPSC. It will integrate the most advanced technologies known to humankind so far. This spacecraft will not only propel humans to Mars, but also other, much further celestial bodies in the future.

LAUNCH VEHICLES

This massive spacecraft will require a massive rocket to propel it into orbit. For this, we designed the most powerful rocket in the world. For this mission, it will be named the Interplanetary Spacecraft Construction Vehicle, or IPSC CV. At a staggering 190 meters tall, 15 meters wide and more than 14,000 tonnes in weight, this monstrous vehicle can put over 400 tonnes into Low Earth Orbit. This rocket will also be used for other heavy payload launches in the future.

MARS COLONY

To allow humans to live and work on Mars, we will establish the first human colony on Mars. Named after the mission, the Ares Mars Colony will house astronauts during their stay on the Red Planet. It will include living rooms, laboratories, rovers, launching and landing pads, ISRU facilities and spacecraft construction facilities.

MARS DESCENT VEHICLE

This will be the vehicle to land the first ever humans on Mars. Currently still under development, the MDV will ensure the safety of our astronauts as they descend onto the Martian surface. Along with the crew, it will also house the 3D printers and other construction systems neccesary to create the Mars Colony.

MARS ASCENT VEHICLE

Upon completion of the surface operations, our crew must re-ascend to the orbiting IPSC. For this, the Mars Ascent Vehicle will be built. Unlike previous missions such as the Apollo and Artemis Missions, the MAV will not be part of the descent vehicle, but will instead be built on Mars from Martian materials. This means that the MDV won't need to carry excess weight on landing, that extra weight being the ascent vehicle.
